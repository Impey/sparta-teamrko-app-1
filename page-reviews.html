Comedy:
Step Brothers

Another unashamedly juvenile comedy from the awe-inspiringly busy Judd Apatow production line: Apatow produces, and the director is Adam McKay, who made the very funny Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy. Will Ferrell and John C Reilly play two 40-year-old guys who live with their respective single parents and have regressed, horribly, to being 13 (a parable, some would say, for the people who make these movies and those who enjoy them). When the boys' long-suffering parents (Richard Jenkins and Mary Steenburgen) happen to meet, fall in love at a medical-technology conference and then get married, Ferrell and Reilly have to share a bedroom as stepbrothers. There are certainly one or two funny, broad moments, especially when the "boys" get it into their heads to convert their beds into bunk beds, and their impersonation of 13-year-olds is scarily accurate. But Reilly has always been more actor than comic, and this goofy comedy is a DVD rental only.

Action:
The Expendables

Stallone! Statham! Willis! Li! Lundgren! Rourke! Schwarzenegger! The lineup makes it sound like it's set in the world's most macho prostate clinic. Sylvester Stallone has called in some favours and created an old-school, pre-Bourne action movie, featuring only pampered beefcakes of a certain age: spiritually located Where Eagles Dare to Fumble for Their Reading Glasses. Sly plays Barney Ross, a cigar-smoking tough guy who heads up a crew of golden-hearted mercenaries. But wait. If you're a Bruce Willis fan, or an Arnie fan, and you're expecting your heroes to have equal screen time with the rest â€“ think again. Willis and Governor Schwarzenegger only get the briefest of cameos at the beginning, but their unsuspecting fanbases will pump up the box office receipts. He's not called "Sly" for nothing, you know: if it was just a diminutive, he'd be "Syl". Anyway, Barney and his compadres are paid to fly in to "Vilena", a Spanish-speaking island somewhere in the "Gulf" and topple a military dictatorship, without worrying over much about leaving behind a stable infrastructure. Poor old Jet Li is mightily dissed with loads of "Chinese people are short" jokes in this lumbering, humourless film, and he never gets to win a single punch-up.
